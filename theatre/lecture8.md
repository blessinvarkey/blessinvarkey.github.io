## Status 
**High Status and Low Status:** 
- Status doesn't necessarily equate with societal roles. Instead, he argues that people play high or low status behaviors in interactions. High status generally makes one appear calm and in control, while low status might make someone seem more submissive or anxious.
- One's status can change in the course of a conversation or interaction. It's not static. For instance, one can start an interaction in a high status position and end in a low status one, or vice versa.
- In the context of theatre, understanding status is crucial for actors to create authentic relationships and dynamics between characters. By adjusting their status in relation to other actors, performers can create tension, humor, and depth.
- Status is often communicated through non-verbal cues. High status might be indicated by a firm stance, direct eye contact, or taking up space, while low status might involve avoiding eye contact, fidgeting, or making oneself smaller.
- The way people speak can also convey status. A firm, steady voice might convey high status, while a hesitant or high-pitched voice might suggest low status.

## Vampire Game (modified for the NIFT lecture)
- The game involves a group of players (we will explore with 2 only) with one of them being secretly designated as the 'vampire'. The other player being the landlord.
- The performers move around the space, interacting with each other.
- Optional end: When a player is "bitten", they don't immediately reveal that they have been touched. Instead, after a short interval (like 10 seconds or so), they'd "die" dramatically.
- Landlord needs to be 'hyper-aware of their surroundings, noting small details and changes.
- The "vampire" has to pick the perfect moment to "bite", and the "bitten" players need to time their "death" well.
- The game is also a lesson in building tension and suspense, qualities essential in good storytelling. The anticipation of who will be bitten next and the mystery of the vampire's identity keep everyone on edge.
- The game can also be seen as a reflection on human behavior – how people react under threat, how suspicion can build, and how individuals respond to changing dynamics in a group setting.
