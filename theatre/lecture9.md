### Scripted Play (25 mintues)
- Review your lines and cues thoroughly.
- Stay in character and maintain the story's continuity.
- Interact authentically with your partner.
- Pay attention to stage directions and movements especially when you will enter the scene.
- Each character has a distinct personality and quirk. Understand your character's specific idiosyncrasy and exaggerate it for comedic effect. Whether it's the mime or the man-hating woman these quirks make the play memorable.
- Comedic timing is essential. The laughs come from the unexpected reactions and sharp exchanges. Practice the transitions between dates and be precise with your delivery.
- This play relies on the chemistry between the two main characters, Guy and Girl. They're the anchors amid the chaos. It's essential to show their growing frustration and disbelief with each subsequent date.
- Many scenes involve a good deal of physical comedy. This needs to be well-rehearsed to appear natural and to ensure safety. 
- Even though the situations are exaggerated, there's a universal theme of the quest for love and the awkwardness of first dates. Make your character relatable. The audience should see a bit of themselves (or someone they know) in each character, which will make the comedy more effective.
- This is a fast-paced play. Keep the energy high throughout, especially during the more outrageous moments.
- Even if you're not the one delivering the punchline, your** reactions are essential**. Comedy often comes from how others respond to the situation.
**- REHERSE YOUR LINES AT ANY COST BEFORE OUR NEXT CLASS!**
