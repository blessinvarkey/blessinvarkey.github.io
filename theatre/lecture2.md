## #2. GE: Theatre (Performance, AI & Design)

>"The story is the hero" - Focus on delivering a well-constructed story than on individual characters/looking better than the other

### How to play
**One Voice**
- The goal of the "One Voice" improv game is to create a scene where all participants speak in perfect unison, as if they have a single voice, while building a coherent and interesting narrative.
- Setup: Group of participants and form a circle or arrange them in a way that everyone can see and hear each other clearly.
- Starting Scene: Choose two participants to start the game. They will initiate a scene together, with one person beginning a sentence or action and the other person immediately joining in, attempting to speak or act in perfect unison with the first person.
- Unison Speaking: The two participants in the starting scene must continue to speak or act in unison, ensuring that their words or actions are perfectly synchronized. They should aim to sound as if they have a single voice.
- **Pass the Scene:** After a short time (e.g., 15-30 seconds), another participant from the circle can "tag in" to join the scene. They must seamlessly join the existing scene, matching the pace and content of the dialogue or actions to maintain the illusion of a single voice.
- **Keep It Going:** The game continues with participants tagging in and out of the scene, trying to maintain the single voice as they build the narrative. Players must pay close attention to the ongoing dialogue and actions to stay synchronized.
- **Challenge:** To make the game more challenging, you can introduce specific themes, scenarios, or characters for the scenes. This can lead to creative and hilarious outcomes as participants work together to maintain the unified voice.
- **Listening and Timing:** Active listening and perfect timing are crucial for the game's success. Participants should react quickly and adjust their speaking or actions to match those of the others in the scene.

**Word at a time**
- The goal of the "Word at a Time" improv game is to collaboratively tell a story, one word at a time, while maintaining a coherent narrative.
- Setup: Two or three participants max.
- Starting Player: Choose one participant to start the game. This player begins by saying the first word of the story. For example, they might say, "Once."
- Pass the Story: After the starting player says their word, the next player in the circle immediately adds the next word to the story. Each player contributes one word at a time, and the story progresses in this manner. For example, the next player might say, "upon," and the player after that says, "a," and so on.
- **Maintain the Narrative:** Players must work together to maintain a coherent narrative as they add one word at a time. The goal is to tell a complete and interesting story. Players can introduce characters, describe settings, and create plot twists as the story evolves.
- **Pay Attention:** Active listening is crucial in this game. Players should pay close attention to the words that have been spoken to ensure that their word fits seamlessly into the story. This helps maintain the flow and coherence of the narrative.
- **Go with the Flow:** The story can take unexpected and humorous turns, especially as players introduce words one at a time. Embrace the spontaneity and use it to your advantage to create a unique and entertaining story.
- **Ending the Story:** The story can continue for a set amount of time, or you can agree as a group on a specific way to conclude it. To end the story, players can work together to craft a satisfying ending sentence.
- **Variations:** To make the game more challenging or creative, you can introduce themes or restrictions, such as telling the story in a specific genre (e.g., science fiction, fairy tale) or setting (e.g., on a deserted island).
- **Practice and Rotate:** You can play multiple rounds, allowing different players to start the story each time. This gives everyone a chance to participate and lead the narrative.

**Three word sentences**
- The goal of the game is to create coherent and entertaining scenes using only three-word sentences.
- Setup: A group of participants form a circle. Choose one player to start the game.
- **Starting Player:** The starting player steps into the center of the circle and begins a scene with a three-word sentence. For example, they might say, "I like cheese."
- **Response:** The player to the left or right (you can decide the direction) of the starting player must respond with their own three-word sentence that continues the scene. For example, they might say, "It's so delicious."
- **Keep It Going:** The game continues with each player taking turns, contributing three-word sentences to build upon the scene. Players must respond quickly and keep the conversation flowing using only three words at a time.
- **Avoid Yes or No:** Participants should avoid using "yes" or "no" in their sentences, as this can make the game too easy. Encourage them to be creative and add details to the scene.
- **No Hesitation:** Players should respond promptly to maintain the rhythm of the game. If a player hesitates or takes too long to respond, they can be eliminated from the round, or you can simply continue without them.
- **Scene Development:** The objective is to create a coherent scene, so players should pay attention to what has been said before and build upon it. The scene can be funny, dramatic, or anything in between, depending on the direction the players take.
